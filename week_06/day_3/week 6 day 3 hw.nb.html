Question 4

bootstrap_95_ci <- bootstrap_sample_100 %>% 
  get_confidence_interval(level = 0.95, type = "percentile")

bootstrap_95_ci

Interpretation

We would expect that 95% of the time our population parameter to lie within the range of this confidence interval of 9898.358 - 10408.25.

Question 5

You would like to know the mean(lot_area) of the sold houses with higher confidence. Calculate the 99% CI for this variable (you can re-use your bootstrap distribution from above). Is it narrower or broader than the 95% CI? Does that make sense?

bootstrap_99_ci <- bootstrap_sample_100 %>% 
  get_confidence_interval(level = 0.99, type = "percentile")

bootstrap_99_ci

Solution

The 99% confidence interval is (slightly) broader than the 95% confidence intervall. This is to be expected since we would expect to capture more sample means within our interval, thus it should be broader.

point_estimate_lot_area
